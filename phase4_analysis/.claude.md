Tier 1 — Do These (High impact, feasible on RTX 4060)
#4 Predictive Coding / Top-Down Feedback — This is essentially what Trial 4 (Multi-Scale Feedback) implements, but framed more precisely. The biological version is Karl Friston's predictive coding: higher areas generate predictions about lower area activations, and only the prediction error (surprise) propagates upward. This is more sophisticated than your current gating approach. Instead of stem = stem + gate * feedback, it becomes stem = stem + gate * (feedback - stem_prediction). That residual error signal is the key. This is the single most biologically grounded improvement you can make.
#8 Neural Representation Alignment — Claude Code is right that this is high impact. The Brain-Score benchmark and the NSD (Natural Scenes Dataset) have publicly available fMRI responses. Adding even a lightweight alignment loss against IT cortex responses would force RHAN's representations to organize the way biological vision does. The challenge is domain mismatch (fMRI is on natural images, you're training on CIFAR-10) but it's solvable with a two-stage approach.
#3 Ventral/Dorsal Stream Split — The "what" vs "where" separation is one of the most empirically validated principles in visual neuroscience. Splitting your transformer into two parallel attention streams — one attending to object identity features, one to spatial relationships — and fusing late is architecturally clean and directly motivated by biology. Adversarial attacks optimized against a single stream would be less effective against the fused output.

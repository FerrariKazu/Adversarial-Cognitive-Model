  2. KEY SCIENTIFIC FINDINGS
================================================================================
  [1] EFFICIENTNET FRAGILITY:
      EfficientNet-B0 achieved the highest clean accuracy (96.8%) but proved
      the most fragile under attack (ε_thresh = 0.006). This supports the
      hypothesis that architectural scaling for clean accuracy often
      comes at the cost of over-reliance on brittle local features.

  [2] RESNET ROBUSTNESS:
      ResNet-18 is the most robust of the tested models (ε_thresh = 0.030).
      Despite being the oldest architecture, its simpler residual blocks
      seem to preserve signal more effectively than the complex scaling
      of EfficientNet or the local patch-binding of BagNet.

  [3] SHAPE-RESNET NEGATIVE RESULT:
      Shape-ResNet-50 (trained on Stylized-ImageNet) did NOT outperform the
      standard ResNet-18 (ε_thresh = 0.008 vs 0.030). This suggests that
      texture-bias is not the only factor in adversarial vulnerability;
      increased depth and filter complexity in the ResNet-50 backbone
      may introduce new vulnerabilities that negate the shape-bias gains.

  [4] ViT LATENT RESILIENCE:
      ViT-Small shows a unique decay curve: rapid initial drop but a very
      long 'tail' of sensitivity at high epsilon. While its threshold is
      lower than ResNet (0.026 vs 0.030), it maintains higher absolute
      accuracy at ε=0.30, suggesting global attention provides a structural
      safety net that local CNNs lack.

  [5] HUMAN BASELINE:
      Human d' remains stable (~2.0) across the entire tested range.
      The baseline 73% accuracy is a result of CIFAR-10 pixelation, not
      perceptual failure. The non-monotonicity observed at ε=0.20 is a
      known sampling limitation in the 100-trial subset.
